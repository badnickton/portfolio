US Technology Stock Analysis

Exploring trends and factors influencing stock prices in the tech sector

About This Project (in progress)

This project investigates trends in the US technology stock market over the past two decades. Using data from the Alpha Vantage API, we explore patterns among major tech companies, sector performance, and factors influencing stock prices.

Project Steps

Step 1: Define Research Questions

We focused on the following key questions:

  • How have US technology stock prices changed over the past two decades?
  • How do sub-sectors like e-commerce and telecommunications compare in performance?
  • What impact do foreign exchange rates have on stock prices?
  • What financial indicators influence stock prices for a company like IBM?

Step 2: Data Collection

We utilized the Alpha Vantage API to gather historical data on stock prices, trading volumes, and financial statements. Data was processed into CSV files to work around API request limits.

Step 3: Analysis Methodology

We conducted the following analyses:

  • Trend Analysis: Examined stock price trends for major companies like Apple, Amazon, and Microsoft.
  • Sector Comparison: Compared performance across sub-sectors like cloud services and telecommunications.
  • Market Correlation: Analyzed correlations between foreign exchange rates and stock prices.
  • Regression Analysis: Assessed IBM's financial indicators to identify key stock price drivers.

Step 4: Key Findings

We observed a general upward trend in tech stock prices, with notable dips during global events like the 2008 financial crisis and the COVID-19 pandemic. Factors such as gross profit, investments, and changes in operating assets positively influenced IBM's stock price, while liabilities and excess inventory had negative effects.
