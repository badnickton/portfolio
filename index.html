UW iSchool Capstone Project: RoMap Relationship Optimization Map

RoMap is a map-first CRM designed for B2B field sales teams who struggle with traditional CRM tools. Field reps think spatially, planning their day by asking “Where have I been?” and “Who should I visit next?”, yet most CRMs force them into lists and heavy manual data entry. Our research found that less than 30% of real sales activity gets logged and 27% of leads are lost due to poor follow-up.

Our team set out to build a CRM that aligns with how field sales actually works. Instead of treating maps as an add on, RoMap makes the map the core workflow. Reps can log visits in seconds, view nearby businesses, and instantly see follow up needs based on proximity and priority. Managers gain real time visibility into territory coverage and rep activity, addressing the need for visibility into rep activity, clear pipeline progress, and territory coverage.

Project Goals

  • Design a CRM that reduces missed follow ups and improves sales pipeline clarity.
  • Create a map first workflow that reflects how field reps naturally plan their day.
  • Validate whether spatial thinking improves adoption and reduces CRM friction.
  • Build a simple, mobile first interface that minimizes data entry burden.

Team Process & Actions

  • Conducted user interviews with field sales reps and managers to understand workflow pain points.
  • Mapped the end to end field sales journey and identified friction points in traditional CRMs.
  • Built a functional prototype including map view, pipeline view, tasks, and team management.
  • Ran usability tests to validate assumptions around spatial workflows and priority vs. proximity.
  • Iterated on design direction, removing gamification after discovering it distorted incentives.

My Individual Contributions

  • Led the design of the map first workflow, ensuring reps could log visits in seconds.
  • Synthesized research insights into actionable design decisions for the team.
  • Developed the presentation materials for our MVP review and refined our narrative.
  • Conducted interviews to validate our ideas.

Key Takeaways

  • Field sales is fundamentally spatial, maps must be the primary interface, not an accessory.
  • Feature overload kills adoption, simplicity is a competitive advantage.
  • Follow up failures are the biggest source of lost revenue, not lack of outreach.
  • Gamification must be designed carefully, bad incentives can harm real sales outcomes.
  • Fast, low friction data entry is essential for any CRM used in the field.
